You can sit back and relax for 10-20 minutes and learn something unique about the way humans work and interact with each other.<\/p>\n<p>The most fascinating talks to me are the ones that focus on how our brains work. You can learn so much about yourself and about others by seeing how our brains react to certain situations.<\/p>\n<p>With knowledge of the human brain, you can become a better person, but you can also learn important lessons that can apply to business.<\/p>\n<p>That&#8217;s what this post is about. I&#8217;ve gone through and found some of my favorite TED Talks as they apply to business. I hope they can be inspiration for you as you work to build something successful for yourself.<\/p>\n<h2>1. Rob Gutman: The Hidden Power Of Smiling<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Ron Gutman: The hidden power of smiling\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/ron_gutman_the_hidden_power_of_smiling\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>I should have been more clear above that I really like TED Talks that discuss psychology studies. This one looks into the psychology and effects of smiling. How often do you smile each day? Do you smile in the morning when you think about seeing your family and friends? Do you smile when you sit down at the desk to start work?<\/p>\n<p>A smile can go a long way to being happy and to leading a successful business full of other happy people. You can be happy all the time (maybe?), but happiness does appear to be a choice.<\/p>\n<p>Oh yeah, people that smile more live longer. Isn&#8217;t that worth a smile or two more each day?<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/ron_gutman_the_hidden_power_of_smiling\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Rob Gutman: The Hidden Power Of Smiling<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>2. Dan Pink: The Puzzle Of Motivation<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Dan Pink: The puzzle of motivation\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/dan_pink_the_puzzle_of_motivation\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>Get your team motivated and you can accomplish anything&#8230;seriously.<\/p>\n<p>If only it were that easy. In this talk, Dan Pink looks at research that could help businesses and managers figure out the keys to human motivation. It&#8217;s the holy grail of the workplace and if you figure it out for your business you could be on the path to growth.<\/p>\n<p>It always seems like we discover much from psychology studies. I love talks like this one that include research on the study of human interaction and inside the human brain.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/dan_pink_on_motivation\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Dan Pink: The Puzzle Of Motivation<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>3. Arianna Huffington: How To Succeed? Get More Sleep.<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Arianna Huffington: How to succeed? Get more sleep\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/arianna_huffington_how_to_succeed_get_more_sleep\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>If you&#8217;ve read the GBW Blog before you know I&#8217;m a <a href=\"https:\/\/ghostblogwriters.com\/startup-productivity-tips\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">big<\/a> <a href=\"https:\/\/ghostblogwriters.com\/small-business-fun\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">proponent<\/a> of getting <a href=\"https:\/\/ghostblogwriters.com\/3-gifts-to-give-yourself-this-year\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">sleep<\/a> each night. In fact, I feel that far too many founders and managers and executives overlook sleep and diet in their executive tool bag. If you&#8217;re not taking care of yourself you&#8217;re giving the competition and advantage and you&#8217;re not giving enough to your customers. Do yourself and your business a favor and get enough sleep each night. It&#8217;s good for your health and for the health of your business.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/arianna_huffington_how_to_succeed_get_more_sleep\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Arianna Huffington: How To Succeed? Get More Sleep.<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>4.Margaret Heffernan: Dare To Disagree<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Margaret Heffernan: Dare to disagree\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/margaret_heffernan_dare_to_disagree\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>This is one of my favorite TED Talks. Margaret is an engaging speaker and the topic is a very interesting one. I&#8217;ve been guilty of being too agreeable in my personal and professional life. I keep things to myself. And I&#8217;m not always quick to disagree with people in the blogging world even though disagreements can lead to some great posts. This is a wonderful talk that can provide inspiration for life and for buisness.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/margaret_heffernan_dare_to_disagree\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Margaret Heffernan: Dare To Disagree<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>5. Shawn Achor: The Happy Secret To Better Work<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Shawn Achor: The happy secret to better work\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/shawn_achor_the_happy_secret_to_better_work\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>More studies. In this talk we hear from a researching that looks into the effect of being happy and good work. You might think that liking your work can lead to happiness, but if you change your attitude you can change your work. It\u2019s a little bit of a Catch-22. I\u2019m a golfer and it\u2019s the same thing with golf. You play better when you\u2019re in a good mood, but you\u2019re not usually in a good mood when you hit a bad shot. Anyway, this is a good talk and it might make you smile a little and that could lead to a better workplace.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/shawn_achor_the_happy_secret_to_better_work\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Shawn Achor: The Happy Secret To Better Work<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>6. John Maeda: Designing For Simplicity<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"John Maeda: Designing for simplicity\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/john_maeda_designing_for_simplicity\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>Why is simplicity in business so difficult? In fact, it seems like we&#8217;re always trying to make things more complex for ourselves in all aspects of our life. We buy more things. We add more to our to-do list. We pile on and pile on. Shouldn&#8217;t we be trying to make things simpler? This is a great talk about design and how being simple can be good for business. It turns out that people do like simple.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/john_maeda_on_the_simple_life\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">John Maeda: Designing For Simplicity<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>7. Susan Cain: The Power Of Introverts<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Susan Cain: The power of introverts\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/susan_cain_the_power_of_introverts\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>I&#8217;m a self-proclaimed introvert. I think I have been for most of my life. I prefer relaxing with a book and glass of wine on Friday night with my fiancee (soon-to-be wife; this summer) rather than going out and being among crowds of people. It&#8217;s not that I don&#8217;t enjoy being out and about from time to time. I just prefer time alone. After going out I need to recharge my batteries. It&#8217;s weird to people that need to be out and amongst crowds. It just kind of works the opposite.<\/p>\n<p>But anyway, this is a great talk from Susan Cain on the power of introverts. I&#8217;m biased, but I think you&#8217;ll like it. Even if you&#8217;re not an introvert you might have some on your team and with this insight you can find their motivation.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/susan_cain_the_power_of_introverts\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Susan Cain: The Power Of Introverts<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>8. Cameron Herold: Let&#8217;s Raise Our Kids To Be Entrepreneurs<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Cameron Herold: Let&#039;s raise kids to be entrepreneurs\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/cameron_herold_let_s_raise_kids_to_be_entrepreneurs\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>Entrepreneurs don&#8217;t often do that well in school. It seems that public schools are setup to make people wonderful employees. That&#8217;s great for some, but not so great for a few others. Entrepreneurs like to break rules. They like to test things and figure out new ways to do things. It&#8217;s a unique trait that is being squashed by normal standards. Raise your kids to be entrepreneurs and you might find that they accomplish great things.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/cameron_herold_let_s_raise_kids_to_be_entrepreneurs?language=en\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Cameron Herold: Let&#8217;s Raise Our Kids To Be Entrepreneurs<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>9. Rory Sutherland: Life Lessons From An Ad Man<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Rory Sutherland: Life lessons from an ad man\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/rory_sutherland_life_lessons_from_an_ad_man\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"1200\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>This TED Talk is very popular. You may have seen it before, but it&#8217;s worth watching again. Those in advertising and sales have such insight into the way business works. They&#8217;re on the front lines seeing how people interact with brands and with products and services. It&#8217;s a wonderful talk and one of the most watched on TED.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch On TED:<\/strong> <a href=\"https:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/rory_sutherland_life_lessons_from_an_ad_man\/transcript?language=en\">Rory Sutherland: Life Lessons From An Ad Man<\/a><\/p>\n<h2>10. Barry Schwartz: The Paradox Of Choice<\/h2>\n<iframe title=\"Barry Schwartz: The paradox of choice\" src=\"https:\/\/embed.ted.com\/talks\/barry_schwartz_the_paradox_of_choice\" sandbox=\"allow-popups allow-scripts allow-same-origin\" width=\"898\" height=\"675\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen><\/iframe>\n<p>We all deal with choice every day. It affects nearly every aspect of our lives. The better we are making choices the better we can make a life that leads to preferable results. We can&#8217;t do it right all the time, but we can learn and adapt our decision making process. In this talk we learn about the complexities of choice. You can apply it to your personal life and how you run your business, but you can also apply it to the way your target customers are thinking.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Watch on TED:<\/strong>\u00a0<a href=\"http:\/\/www.ted.com\/talks\/barry_schwartz_on_the_paradox_of_choice\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Barry Schwartz: The Paradox Of Choice<\/a><\/p>\n<p><em>Now it&#8217;s your turn.
