Here\u2019s what everyone\u2019s really thinking about online marketing and what you really need to know to find your way.<\/p>\n<h2>What Do We Really Need to Know About Digital Marketing?<\/h2>\n<p>All buzz words aside, digital marketing and online strategies are relevant. Actually, if you\u2019re not online, you\u2019re soon to be extinct. And by online, we don\u2019t just mean having a website. Having a beautiful website won\u2019t increase your bottom line if you\u2019re not doing anything to promote it. It\u2019s the digital marketing strategy that will attract visitors to your site and, ultimately, your products or services. Without it, you\u2019ll be obsolete, especially in today\u2019s commerce.<\/p>\n<p>Here are <a href=\"https:\/\/www.hubspot.com\/marketing-statistics\">some HubSpot stats<\/a> to support the critical need for digital marketing:<\/p>\n<ul>\n<li>A website is the second most popular channel used in online marketing, with social media being the dominant channel for customer engagement.<\/li>\n<li>69% of all marketers invested in SEO efforts in 2021<\/li>\n<li>71% of all marketers indicated \u201cstrategic keywords\u201d were the primary SEO factor in 2021<\/li>\n<li>3.5% of eCommerce web visits using mobile devices converted into online purchases, while 3.9% converted using a desktop application.<\/li>\n<li>52% of consumers using a \u201cvoice assistant\u201d admit to using them daily, sometimes several times a day<\/li>\n<li>The average American consumer has more than ten \u201cconnected devices\u201d in their homes as of 2021<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<p>So, yes, you really need to know about digital marketing. It\u2019s where you\u2019ll find and attract buyers. And now that you know you <a href=\"https:\/\/ghostblogwriters.com\/how-to-build-a-home-run-sales-funnel\/\">need to develop a strategy<\/a>, you can take a look at your current positioning and online presence now to create a roadmap for improvements.<\/p>\n<h2>How Can I Tell What Advice Makes Sense for My Business?<\/h2>\n<p>Knowing you need online marketing is one thing. But what everyone\u2019s really thinking is, how can I tell what digital marketing advice is right for my business?<\/p>\n<p>The short answer is you\u2019ll just have to do your own homework.<\/p>\n<p>Yes, there are best practices out there that make sense for every business model. But there are also so-called experts out there advising marketers and business owners to buy ads, run campaigns, and invest in lofty strategies that may not necessarily be the right fit.<\/p>\n<p>The only way to really know which advice is legitimate for your bottom line is by conducting your own analytics and metrics research. Based on your company\u2019s efforts now, look to identify who your target buyers are, what methods you are using to generate leads, where customers find you the most, and what conversion rates you\u2019re experiencing now. Use your precise data as a starting point for exploring solutions, improvements, and new ways to tap into digital marketing.<\/p>\n<p>You\u2019re likely more of an expert in your business than you are in digital marketing strategies. So, with your data in hand, it\u2019s absolutely recommended that you find a partner to help you put together a roadmap and plan. Just make sure you work with a marketing partner that will listen intently to your ideas and who will understand what your company needs. Be thorough when vetting marketing partners so you don\u2019t end up being \u201csold\u201d a slew of expensive or unnecessary online marketing investments that won\u2019t <a href=\"https:\/\/ghostblogwriters.com\/5-traits-of-a-successful-entrepreneur-in-2022\/\">generate the results you need<\/a>.<\/p>\n<h2>How Much Is This Digital Marketing Really Going to Cost Me?<\/h2>\n<p>Speaking of expensive, you\u2019re now wondering how much is it really going to cost? Here\u2019s the thing. It\u2019s only a waste of money if it doesn\u2019t work. Therefore, the key to understanding a fair investment is first identifying how much a lead or customer is worth to you. Customers have an immediate value as well as a lifetime value, too. Would it be worth it to spend $50 to get one new customer for a lifetime of purchases? What about $500? Or $5,000?<\/p>\n<p>If you\u2019re spending on campaigns that aren\u2019t generating leads, then stop. If you\u2019re investing in something that is generating leads, but you\u2019re struggling to convert those leads, it\u2019s another problem altogether. Whatever you do, don\u2019t stop online marketing. That\u2019s a must. You may instead just need to realign your investments with strategies that produce the results you\u2019re needing to justify the investment.<\/p>\n<p>Remember, if you had an unlimited budget, you could afford to do it all. But if you\u2019re like most companies, budgets are tight. That\u2019s why it\u2019s even more critical that you be precise about where you allocate it.<\/p>\n<h2>I Can\u2019t Afford to Guess My Way to Results<\/h2>\n<p>You don\u2019t have to, nor want to, guess your way through digital marketing strategies if you make smart use of your metrics ongoing. What you don\u2019t want to do, and no matter how much you feel like doing it, is throw up your hands and fling ideas to the wall to see what sticks. That\u2019s a surefire way to waste money.<\/p>\n<p>And you don\u2019t want to be biting off more than you can chew either, meaning you don\u2019t have to invest in every online channel, chase every shiny new digital marketing object, or be on every social platform to see success. Instead, sample a few channels or ideas at a time. Invest enough to make them worthwhile and work them diligently. Monitor the data to see what\u2019s working. And much like you would approach changing your diet when your doctor says your cholesterol\u2019s too high, add and subtract small changes at a time. See what works. See what fails. Explore new improvements. And when you find something that translates to more leads and conversions, dominate it!<\/p>\n<h2>Who Has Time for All This Digital Marketing?<\/h2>\n<p>That\u2019s a lot of work. You\u2019re running a business. Do you really have time to babysit social media posts, blogging schedules, or Google Ad campaign metrics? Don\u2019t overextend yourself, risking burnout or oversights. Recognize that when you embark on a digital marketing journey, it\u2019s likely time to get help managing your efforts.<\/p>\n<p>Depending on your organization&#8217;s size and the nature of your industry, you might be best served by partnering with an outside strategist to help. But there should come a point when you need to hire internally to have a dedicated staff member responsible for posting, managing, and monitoring results. Once you start an online marketing plan, you can\u2019t stop. And whether you&#8217;re failing or succeeding, you are going to need help to fix and scale your results.<\/p>\n<h2>How Do I Know I Need Help with My Digital Marketing?<\/h2>\n<p>If you\u2019re confident you have a good handle on developing, implementing, managing, adjusting, and evaluating your entire digital marketing plan on your own, you\u2019re on the verge of needing help. If you\u2019re growing and scaling, your strategies will only need to grow and adapt with you. Get ahead and make sure you have the tools, resources, and staff in place to maintain that momentum.<\/p>\n<p>On the flip side, if you\u2019re struggling, you need help, too. Don\u2019t take it personally. There\u2019s so much to unpack and plenty of changing elements involved. Be smart and recognize when you need a hand, and take the steps to get the help you need. Your bottom line depends on it.<\/p>\n<p>Here\u2019s what everyone really thinks about online marketing. UX\/UI Designs, AI, PPC, CPV, and what? There are too many acronyms to keep up with every trend, every tool, and every resource. The good news is, as a business owner, you don\u2019t have to know and understand it all.<\/p>\n<p>However, what does matter, is that you know what you don\u2019t know and lean on the professionals who do know. If these acronyms are foreign territory for you, it\u2019s time to bring in an expert to help.<\/p>\n<h2>Can\u2019t I Just Stick with My Vehicle Wraps and Yard Signs?<\/h2>\n<p>Just because digital marketing is an essential pillar of your business doesn\u2019t mean you have to abandon your non-digital marketing efforts. Look, if it works, keep doing it. And there are certain business models that absolutely rely on traditional channels like mailers, vehicle wraps, and signage. And trade shows are downright money makers for some companies.<\/p>\n<p>With that being said, there are ways to use digital marketing to get more traction with those non-digital efforts. And, of course, if you\u2019re not getting leads from your yard signs, they\u2019re probably not helping much. So, if you have a really great offline marketing technique, consider boosting it with online marketing promotion. Share video and images of your stunningly wrapped service vans on social media. Use your online channels to help promote an upcoming mass mailer. But remember, non-digital efforts alone won\u2019t likely sustain the results your business needs to grow.<\/p>\n<h2>Social Media Is Too Much Time, Effort, and Drama, Isn\u2019t It?<\/h2>\n<p>You have a hard enough time keeping up with your personal social media profiles. How are you supposed to know what to do or have the time to do it with a company social media presence? And with the experts suggesting that you need to post regularly, do live videos, and boost ads, that\u2019s more time than you have to dedicate. Never mind the dramatic nature of the social ecosystem, and you\u2019ve heard horror stories of other small businesses struggling with trolls and terrible online reviews.<\/p>\n<p>Wouldn\u2019t it just be best to avoid social altogether? No.<\/p>\n<p>You really do need it. And there is a right and a wrong way to do it effectively. Like all your other digital strategies, you don\u2019t have to do every social channel to be successful, and you don\u2019t want to overextend beyond what you can manage. Find out which social media channels are most popular for your target customers first. Then look to be engaging on one or two platforms by offering content in the form of posts, images, and videos that are less about asking for the sale and more about solving customer problems and aligning your brand in their minds.<\/p>\n<h2>Does Blogging Really Work?<\/h2>\n<p>With campaigns, online ads, messaging, social media, and metrics, the experts say you should also consider blogging. And you\u2019re probably thinking, really?<\/p>\n<p>No one is any more of an expert in your business than you are. And believe it or not, you have really important ideas worth sharing and solutions worth discussing. Your audience would love to hear more. But you don\u2019t necessarily have time to sit down and reflect and write thoughtful essays. And for some, there\u2019s some anxiety about writing anything for fear it won\u2019t be well-received or put together.<\/p>\n<p>Blogging and thought-leadership articles are huge assets in your online marketing toolbox. They can help position your brand as an authority in your space. And it provides you with a library of digital assets from which you can cross-promote and cross-post elsewhere online. You can repurpose old topics with fresh views. You can share unique ideas and new solutions. And while blogs and articles might not result in immediate lead generation, they will create a movement of awareness and brand identity that can certainly translate to better lead gen over time.<\/p>\n<p>Like every other aspect of online marketing, you can\u2019t blog part-time. It\u2019s a snowball that will only make an impact if you\u2019re able to keep it rolling.
