When we forget this, bad things happen, because we end up serving our routine without any sense of why we\u2019re doing it.<\/p>\n<p>Change is critical to your well-being and even to your productivity. Here we\u2019ll look at a few small, practical shifts you can make that will make a big difference.<\/p>\n<h2>1. Take More Breaks<\/h2>\n<p>The busier you get, the more projects you add to your queue, the longer your to-do list, the greater the temptation there will be to stretch out your workday to accommodate pressing deadlines.<\/p>\n<p>But <a href=\"https:\/\/workplace.msu.edu\/breaks-during-the-workday\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">study<\/a> after study shows this could be more detrimental to your productivity than you even realize.<\/p>\n<p>Taking breaks at regular intervals can boost your productivity, increase your creativity, prevent decision fatigue, and more.<\/p>\n<p>Although taking more breaks might seem like the opposite of what you should do when your schedule is bursting at the seams, it may well be the key to accomplishing more in your day.<\/p>\n<h2>2. Add Some Movement to Your Day<\/h2>\n<p>It\u2019s easy to get caught up in an all or nothing mindset when it comes to exercise. \u201cWell, if I\u2019m going to do it anyway, I may as well go all out and push for a two-hour full body workout.\u201d<\/p>\n<p>And the catch is that, when we think that way, we often end up doing nothing. We either can\u2019t find the time or are too exhausted by the end of the day to try to squeak in a workout.<\/p>\n<p>You\u2019re much better off going for a walk during your breaks, knocking out a 10- to 15-minute HIIT workout in the morning, or engaging in some other hobby you can easily pick up \u2013 basketball, fishing, skateboarding, surfing (depending on your locality), and so on.<\/p>\n<p>You\u2019ll catch some rays, get your blood flowing, your sense of self-confidence will increase, and you\u2019ll feel refreshed to tackle the rest of your day.<\/p>\n<h2>3. Change Your Environment<\/h2>\n<p>If you have the flexibility of working remotely, then it might be to your advantage to mix things up on occasion.<\/p>\n<p>The monotony of working at the same desk doing the same things at the same time day in day out can wear on even the most disciplined and prolific of individuals.<\/p>\n<p>Changing your environment can shift your mood and inspire new ideas. The trick will be to find a space that works for you, whether it\u2019s the library, Starbucks, or a pub. Find a space that adds to your vibrancy and doesn\u2019t distract you from your work.<\/p>\n<h2>4. Schedule Something Fun<\/h2>\n<p>Have something to look forward to in your week. If your days are filled with nothing but work, no matter how much you love your work, you will start to wonder what all the sacrifice is for.<\/p>\n<p>Let your inner child out. Go out and play. You will always have more work to do, but time is one thing you can\u2019t recover. Once it\u2019s spent, it\u2019s gone.
