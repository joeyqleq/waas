the ideals of persistence and perseverance were reinforced as worthy ideals.<\/p>\n<p>Yet, there isn\u2019t as much virtue in staying in unfavorable, miserable, or downright toxic situations as people seem to think there is.<\/p>\n<p>Fundamentally, we are all after a feeling we think we will get when we obtain something. And if we were to accept that as true, following one\u2019s bliss truly is the only thing to prioritize in life.<\/p>\n<p>So, is it time to step away to pursue a new path?<\/p>\n<h2>Are You Exhausted?<\/h2>\n<p>Push the body to its limits. Drink caffeine. Work 16-hour days. Give up social events. Sacrifice sleep. Anyone who plays the \u201csacrifice game\u201d long enough will burn themselves out, and it will probably happen sooner than they think.<\/p>\n<p>But the only type of work that truly exhausts you is the work you resist. If you are resisting something, you have one of two options:<\/p>\n<ul>\n<li aria-level=\"1\">You can change your context for the work and approach it from a fresh perspective (you\u2019ll probably need to step away from it for a couple of weeks to be able to gain a new perspective).<\/li>\n<li aria-level=\"1\">You can choose something new that gives you energy and step away from what is stealing your energy.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<p>Not everything is guaranteed to go right just because you\u2019re doing something you love, but on balance, you will be resisting less, and as a result, you won\u2019t be exhausted all the time either.<\/p>\n<h2>Are You Excited About Something Else?<\/h2>\n<p>Just because you\u2019re flirting with the new and exciting doesn\u2019t necessarily mean it\u2019s the right path for you. Simultaneously, excitement will always accompany the path best suited for you.<\/p>\n<p>If you\u2019ve been despising your current path for years while taking an interest in other passions or potential business ventures, then you have all the evidence you need already. The time to take a new path is now.<\/p>\n<p>Your new idea will probably take everything you\u2019ve got to execute, so you don\u2019t have the luxury of being double-minded. You\u2019ve got to take a leap of faith to fulfill your new vision.<\/p>\n<h2>Do You Have a Hunch?<\/h2>\n<p>Perhaps the most difficult aspect of stepping away to pursue a new path is that no one else can tell you when or when not to do it.<\/p>\n<p>Your family and friends want the best for you. Or, it might be more accurate to say they want the <i>safest <\/i>path for you. So, if you were to ask them for advice, there\u2019s a good chance they will tell you to stay the course and \u201chang in there\u201d no matter how miserable you might be.<\/p>\n<p>This has proven bad advice for countless budding entrepreneurs who\u2019ve chosen the safer path instead of going with their gut.<\/p>\n<p>The entrepreneurial journey is rarely easy, but it is almost always the more fulfilling.<\/p>\n<p>If you have a hunch you\u2019re supposed to be doing something else, you probably should be entertaining those thoughts. Circumstances may not appear to favor you now, but there is no such thing as perfect timing, and if not now, then when?<\/p>\n<h2>Closing Thoughts<\/h2>\n<p>Andrew Hitz of The Entrepreneurial Musician says he has yet to find a single example of people who followed their heart and stepped away from seemingly amazing careers to do something else and have ultimately regretted it. They\u2019ve always come out on top.<\/p>\n<p>It\u2019s easier said than done, but there may come a time when you need to step away to pursue a new path.
