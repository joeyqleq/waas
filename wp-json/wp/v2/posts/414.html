I knew things couldn&#8217;t have been caused by the writing I was doing. Usually when a spike happens it&#8217;s the result of something extra happening.<\/p>\n<h2>Google Insights for Blog Tracking<\/h2>\n<p>I went to Google Insights and checked out the search traffic trend for the term &#8220;Country Music&#8221;.<\/p>\n<p><script type=\"text\/javascript\" src=\"http:\/\/www.gmodules.com\/ig\/ifr?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.google.com%2Fig%2Fmodules%2Fgoogle_insightsforsearch_interestovertime_searchterms.xml&amp;up__property=empty&amp;up__search_terms=country+music&amp;up__location=US&amp;up__category=0&amp;up__time_range=12-m&amp;up__compare_to_category=false&amp;synd=open&amp;w=500&amp;h=350&amp;lang=en-US&amp;title=Google+Insights+for+Search&amp;border=%23ffffff%7C3px%2C1px+solid+%23999999&amp;output=js\"><\/script><\/p>\n<p>As you can see the traffic for the term spikes in April. There is also a big award show in the industry &#8211; the ACM Awards. This occurs the first week of April each year. I&#8217;m sure a little traffic spike is caused by people searching for award related terms.<\/p>\n<p>I assumed in general if there was a spike in country music there would be a spike in all terms country music related. This is likely the cause of at least some of the extra traffic heading CML&#8217;s way over the last week and even the last month. Hopefully it continues through April.<\/p>\n<h3>Summary<\/h3>\n<p>Understanding the trends in search traffic can help you prepare for variations in your traffic and even revenue statistics. It can help you plan when you may get more or fewer leads throughout the year. Understanding when spikes occur or even low periods can help ease the cycle of excitement and disappointment as you blog.<\/p>\n<p>Tracking daily visits can drive you nuts when things are up and down over time.
