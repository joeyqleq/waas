We get these little urges and ideas and our minds run wild.<\/p>\n<p>But you can have all the dreams you want, but in the end you&#8217;ll be what you do every day.<\/p>\n<h3>Changing Jobs And Starting Businesses<\/h3>\n<p>So I struggled with this one for a bit in a few different ways.<\/p>\n<p>I had a great job that I liked, but I also had a few ideas for different businesses. One ended up being Ghost Blog Writers, but it started out really as nothing.<\/p>\n<p>I had been reading blogs. I figured I could do one, but never got started. Then one day I realized that if I wanted to be a blogger I just had to start and then keep doing it. I figured I&#8217;d get there eventually; learn along the way.<\/p>\n<p>Then I got asked to blog for a company and then another and it grew from there.<\/p>\n<p>Then the next time I was growing the business on the side and reached a point where I had to make a choice. Keep doing that or leave my job and become a real business owner full-time.<\/p>\n<p>I had to become what I wanted to be.<\/p>\n<h3>Getting Away From Writing<\/h3>\n<p>I don&#8217;t do any writing for clients at GBW anymore. A few years ago I had to make that jump. I wanted to be a business owner. Blogging is still something I enjoy doing, but I wanted to ultimately be a business owner.<\/p>\n<p>So I had to make the step of becoming what I did every day. I had to move away from being a blogger and a writer and hire great people to do that instead and move on to the next stage.<\/p>\n<h3>New Challenges<\/h3>\n<p>Now it&#8217;s also getting to the point where I am what I do every day. Right now that&#8217;s really the role of account manager. And it&#8217;s been great, but now it&#8217;s time to move on again.<\/p>\n<p>I want to be a business owner. I want the business to run on its own without me. So I&#8217;ll have to make a step where I am what I do every day and become something other than an account manager.<\/p>\n<h3>Hobbies<\/h3>\n<p>I think we all also have hobbies that we want to do as well. We don&#8217;t have time for everything, but we do have time for a few things.<\/p>\n<p>But if you really want to get good at something you have to do it every day. For me that&#8217;s golf. And I notice it and don&#8217;t do as well as I should.<\/p>\n<p>When I practice even if it&#8217;s just practice swings or holding a club or putting. It helps.<\/p>\n<p>I follow Hank Haney and he talks about it all the time. He had a TV show where he helped celebrities become better golfers and he worked with Michael Phelps. Michael said that with swimming if he misses a day of practice it takes him like two days to get back to where he was.<\/p>\n<p>I think Ben Hogan said the same thing about golf practice.<\/p>\n<p>We are what we do every day.<\/p>\n<h3>Final Thought<\/h3>\n<p>If you want to become something you have to adjust what you do every day. You have to dedicate time to something if you want it to happen. It won&#8217;t just happen and I think we all know that.<\/p>\n<p>But getting started is the first step. That&#8217;s a difficult step, but it&#8217;s actually easier than doing something every day.<\/p>\n<p>Whatever you want to do or become you have to commit to doing it every day or it won&#8217;t happen.
