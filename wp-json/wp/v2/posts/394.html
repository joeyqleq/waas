The main term I was targeting for this post was &#8220;<a href=\"http:\/\/www.google.com\/search?source=ig&amp;hl=en&amp;rlz=&amp;q=best+garth+brooks+songs&amp;aq=f&amp;aqi=p-p1g-v1&amp;aql=&amp;oq=&amp;pws=0\">best garth brooks songs<\/a>&#8220;. It&#8217;s not a huge traffic term, but there is some traffic there. CML now has a good ranking for that term &#8211; #1 and sometimes #1, #2, and #3 on a good day.<\/p>\n<p>Great news.<\/p>\n<p>The interesting thing is that first position ranking generated a total of 321 pageviews. The top keyword generated just over 25% of the total keyword traffic for this post.<\/p>\n<p>Where did the rest come from?<\/p>\n<p>Longtail keywords.<\/p>\n<h1>Do Not Ignore The Longtail<\/h1>\n<p>Getting the top ranking for a pretty good term was great, but it&#8217;s striking that only 25% of the traffic to the Garth Brooks post came from the main term I was targeting. The other 75% was coming from a mix of 165 or so other longtail keyword phrases.<\/p>\n<p>This post was an early example and I wrote it early on after the launch of CML. I actually didn&#8217;t target the long tail on purpose. It took me a while to catch on. Search engines were picking up longtail keywords from within the post in the content I had written like &#8220;top garth brooks songs of all time&#8221;. Somewhere in the post I had used the word &#8220;top&#8221; and search engines picked it up.<\/p>\n<p>Over the months after the Garth post I started realizing the importance of the longtail. SEO experts have been talking about it for a long time &#8211; it just took me a few months to realize.<\/p>\n<p>For posts like <a href=\"http:\/\/countrymusiclife.com\/sad-country-songs\/\">Sad Country Songs<\/a> I included words like Depressing, Saddest, and Lonely in the title tag. Now that song gets half its traffic from &#8220;sad country songs&#8221; and the other half from longtail words including &#8220;lonely country songs&#8221; and &#8220;depressing country songs&#8221;.<\/p>\n<p>The same is true for posts like <a href=\"http:\/\/countrymusiclife.com\/chris-young-tomorrow\/\">Chris Young Tomorrow<\/a>. A third of the traffic for this term comes from &#8220;Chris Young Tomorrow&#8221;, another few percent from &#8220;Tomorrow Chris Young&#8221; and the rest from longtail words. Along the way I realized that adding the words &#8220;Listen&#8221;, &#8220;Download&#8221;, and occasionally &#8220;video&#8221; and &#8220;song&#8221; work well for longtail terms.<\/p>\n<p><strong>Summary<\/strong><\/p>\n<p>It&#8217;s been interesting testing stuff with longtail keywords.<\/p>\n<p>After messing around there are a few formulas that work right now for CML. I&#8217;m sure things will change over time, which requires constant testing.<\/p>\n<p>It&#8217;s fun and the lesson is&#8230;<\/p>\n<p>Don&#8217;t ignore the longtail.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"<p>I&#8217;ve been messing around with longtail keyword phrases on Country Music Life for a few months and things have been interesting.
