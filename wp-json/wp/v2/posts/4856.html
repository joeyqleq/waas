It&#8217;s more about trying new things because if you stand still in life you&#8217;re going to get passed.<\/p>\n<p>In my life, there have definitely been times when I&#8217;ve worried about what other people would think. I don&#8217;t know what it is, but we have a culture that kind of focuses on the things people don&#8217;t do well.<\/p>\n<p>For example, when a favorite music act tries something a little different on an album. It comes out and it&#8217;s not what fans expect or want. The album is panned and the act might have a bad reputation for the rest of their career.<\/p>\n<p>That happens throughout life and it seems that as we get older we get more fearful of trying new things for fear of failure and ridicule.<\/p>\n<p>I&#8217;ve tried to fight those fears in my life.<\/p>\n<h2>Blogging<\/h2>\n<p>In 2009, I started blogging for the first time. I had no idea what I was doing. I broke a lot of unwritten rules in the blogging world. I read a lot of blogs and figured I could do it, but I had no idea what I was doing.<\/p>\n<p>I didn&#8217;t really tell a lot of people about it for fear that they would make fun of what I was trying to do. In fact, I had no idea what I was trying to do. I was just experimenting with something that I had some interest in and that seemed to be popular with others.<\/p>\n<p>Flash forward a few years and now blogging is my life&#8217;s work. Well, it&#8217;s my life&#8217;s work up to this point. We have Ghost Blog Writers, which I love having and love managing.<\/p>\n<p>It&#8217;s save to say that blogging as an experiment has panned out so far.<\/p>\n<h2>Podcasting And Video<\/h2>\n<p>Now I&#8217;m experimenting with podcasting. My wife and I will be putting out commentary on business, design, content and what it&#8217;s like to both work from home and to have a husband and wife with their own small businesses.<\/p>\n<p>We don&#8217;t really have any expectations, but we&#8217;re trying it. We&#8217;ll probably be horrible starting out, but I&#8217;m excited to see where it could go and what opportunity it could bring.<\/p>\n<p>Nothing might come of it, but that would be okay too. It&#8217;s just an experiment.<\/p>\n<p>A couple years ago I also started filming my deer hunts. This year I&#8217;m going to get back into doing that regularly again. I hope it turns out to be worthwhile. So far it&#8217;s just a hobby and I&#8217;m fine with it being that way. It&#8217;s another experiment.<\/p>\n<h2>Expectations<\/h2>\n<p>I think it&#8217;s fine to go into experimenting with expectations. But as an example, a friend of mine commented on my early blogging days by asking, &#8220;You&#8217;re blogging? What good can come of that? What&#8217;s in it for you?&#8221;<\/p>\n<p>He was talking about money. I really had no idea if money would come of it. I was just trying something.<\/p>\n<p>And I think that if you&#8217;re good at something and if you pay attention to what people need then you can figure out the money side of things.<\/p>\n<p>We&#8217;ll see what podcasting and video bring, but right now I have no expectation of money. There are greater rewards in life.<\/p>\n<p>All I know is that experimenting in life and in business have worked out well for me so far.
