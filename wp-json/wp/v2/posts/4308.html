{"id":4308,"date":"2014-03-17T10:00:19","date_gmt":"2014-03-17T15:00:19","guid":{"rendered":"https:\/\/ghostblogwriters.com\/?p=4308"},"modified":"2014-03-17T10:00:19","modified_gmt":"2014-03-17T15:00:19","slug":"startup-productivity-tips","status":"publish","type":"post","link":"https:\/\/ghostblogwriters.com\/startup-productivity-tips\/","title":{"rendered":"5 Productivity Tips For Startup Founders"},"content":{"rendered":"<figure id=\"attachment_3792\" aria-describedby=\"caption-attachment-3792\" style=\"width: 290px\" class=\"wp-caption alignright\"><img fetchpriority=\"high\" decoding=\"async\" class=\"size-medium wp-image-3792\" alt=\"Dayne Shuda\" src=\"https:\/\/ghostblogwriters.com\/wp-content\/uploads\/2013\/10\/Dayne-Shuda-2013-300x225.jpg\" width=\"300\" height=\"225\" \/><figcaption id=\"caption-attachment-3792\" class=\"wp-caption-text\">Take a step back and turn your startup around.<\/figcaption><\/figure>\n<p>Are you confused why your startup isn&#8217;t gaining any traction?<\/p>\n<p>Many startup founders find that they&#8217;re treading water. They&#8217;re busy all the time and out of energy. It seems like they&#8217;re pouring everything into the business without getting anything in return.<\/p>\n<p>It&#8217;s a common situation and I&#8217;ve been there with Ghost Blog Writers. In the past, I&#8217;ve worn myself out working endless hours while it seemed like I wasn&#8217;t making any progress on actually growing the business. It&#8217;s frustrating and it can drain your passion for the company. And that&#8217;s really not good because it makes you feel like giving it up.<\/p>\n<p>Here are my best startup productivity tips for startup founders. These tips should actually help you grow more while seemingly doing less.<\/p>\n<h2>1. Set Your Key Business Activities<\/h2>\n<p>This is the biggest one I&#8217;ve had to learn and I&#8217;m still working on it. When you&#8217;re busy all day, every day and you&#8217;re not getting anywhere it&#8217;s usually the result of working on the wrong things. You might think that what you&#8217;re working on is important, but how important can it be if you&#8217;re not growing?<\/p>\n<p>I&#8217;ve learned that getting customers is the only way to grow a business. You can work on your product all day long, but if you&#8217;re not getting anyone to use it then you&#8217;re not going to succeed.<\/p>\n<p>About a year ago I was working on client blog posts at GBW. It was an important task, but it wasn&#8217;t the top priority. A top priority was getting customers. So I decided that for the business to grow I would need to delegate that task and focus more on marketing and sales. Now I put more effort into the posts on the GBW Blog, which brings in our inbound leads and I work on closing those leads when they come in.<\/p>\n<p>Figure out how you can get new customers and make sure either you or someone else focuses on it every day.<\/p>\n<h2>2. Delegate All Repeatable Tasks<\/h2>\n<p>Every task is possible to delegate. For about a year I&#8217;ve been working on setting up repeatable systems for GBW. So far I&#8217;ve been able to delegate all the client work and it&#8217;s been great for the business. It was scary at first, but the business has been able to grow as a result.<\/p>\n<p>Now, I&#8217;m trying to systemize the marketing and sales processes along with the managing of the business on the accounting side of things.<\/p>\n<p>The trap for startup founders is to think that you have to do everything in the business. I&#8217;ve fallen into it. Now my mindset is on making it possible for someone else to do all the tasks. I don&#8217;t want to be a necessary requirement for GBW to run effectively. I want to be able to take vacations and know that the business can run and grow without me.<\/p>\n<p>Start creating processes for every task in your business starting with the ones you don&#8217;t like and the ones that are most important. For me, it was delegating client blog writing. Next it&#8217;s delegating marketing, sales and accounting.<\/p>\n<h2>3. Schedule Every Phone Call<\/h2>\n<p>If you know me you know that I&#8217;m not a huge fan of the phone. I think it&#8217;s easier to get things done via email, but what I&#8217;ve learned is that I don&#8217;t necessarily hate talking on the phone. What I hate is being interrupted with calls out of the blue. I like to have a plan when I talk on the phone. Being prepared makes the call much more effective.<\/p>\n<p>And I like to schedule my days with the highest priority items first each day. If I get a call while I&#8217;m working through my schedule I don&#8217;t answer it. I used to and it drove me nuts. I would struggle to get things done. Now I schedule every call for after noon. If someone calls I&#8217;ll let them leave a message. Then I&#8217;ll email them and provide a few times for the discussion. I&#8217;ll make these times for later in the day so they&#8217;re after I get the top priority items done first.<\/p>\n<p>Yes, some businesses will have to put out fires and take phone calls on demand, but this is where you&#8217;ll need to setup a process for someone to handle the task of taking phone calls. If it&#8217;s a priority item then setup a process and make it someone&#8217;s job to handle it.<\/p>\n<h2>4. Get On A Proper Diet And Exercise Plan<\/h2>\n<p>These last two are a little different than you&#8217;ve probably read on other posts, but I think they&#8217;re extremely important. The first is diet and exercise. Yes, we all know that diet and exercise are good, but it seems that &#8220;busy&#8221; startup owners are really bad with their diet and exercise. They each &#8220;easy&#8221; junk food and sit in their chair all day.<\/p>\n<p>I didn&#8217;t want to fall into this so I make sure to take walks 4-5 days a week when possible. I take the stairs. I also do high intensity workouts (<a href=\"http:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=zy7j9FRiJpg\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">video<\/a>) 2 times each week along with basic weight training (push-ups, planks, etc.).<\/p>\n<p>It doesn&#8217;t take much. The intensity workouts and weight training takes one hour\u00a0<em>a week<\/em>. The walking can be done in 20, 30 or 60 minutes each time depending and walking is a great way to think about your business&#8217;s big picture. You can put the walking on auto-pilot and think about your business.<\/p>\n<p>Next, <a href=\"http:\/\/www.marksdailyapple.com\/welcome-to-marks-daily-apple\/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">cut out the sugar and carbs<\/a>. They cause your <a href=\"http:\/\/www.nytimes.com\/2012\/07\/01\/opinion\/sunday\/what-really-makes-us-fat.html?_r=0\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">body to get fat<\/a> and they also sap you of energy and <a href=\"http:\/\/www.kurzweilai.net\/high-carb-foods-associated-with-cognitive-impairment-mayo-clinic-study\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">brain power<\/a>. If you&#8217;re serious about the success of your business then you owe it to yourself to eat the right food that will give you the most possible brain power.<\/p>\n<h2>5. Let Yourself Get Good Sleep Every Night<\/h2>\n<p>Finally, it&#8217;s time to break the idea that there is no time to sleep. If you&#8217;re not sleeping then you&#8217;re ridding yourself of the energy and health you need to be at your best. There is a stigma in the business world that it&#8217;s good to work all the time. It&#8217;s like a rite of passage to work 20 hour days for a full week.<\/p>\n<p>Stop it.<\/p>\n<p>Get 8 hours of sleep each night. Plan your next day so you&#8217;re not thinking about your business when you go to bed. Read stories before bed time to get your mind on something else and drift off to sleep.<\/p>\n<p>I&#8217;ve found that I do much better work if I put a task off until tomorrow and do it when I wake up refreshed. If I try to kill it late into the night I do worse work on it and become extremely tired the next day.<\/p>\n<h2>Conclusion<\/h2>\n<p>I&#8217;ve been there. It&#8217;s difficult to remove yourself from certain aspects of your startup business, but it&#8217;s necessary if you want to get your life back and to grow your business. You simply can&#8217;t do everything and most of the time it&#8217;s easy to get caught up doing things that aren&#8217;t even important.<\/p>\n<p>Follow this advice and your business should grow and you should get your health and happiness back too.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"<p>Are you doing everything for your startup?
