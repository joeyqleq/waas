But it wasn&#8217;t just a free for all. We had a list of chores to do before our parents got back home.<\/p>\n<p>My thinking then and still today was to get those chores done right away in the morning. This way I didn&#8217;t have to think about the thing I didn&#8217;t want to do all day and once I was done I had the rest of the day to do what I wanted.<\/p>\n<p>Even today I still do that with work. There are things that have to get done, but that I don&#8217;t necessarily enjoy and I try to do those right in the morning. Then I have the rest of the day to do the more enjoyable items on the to-do list.<\/p>\n<p>But I&#8217;m starting to think that maybe this is backwards&#8230;at least sideways when it comes to running a business.<\/p>\n<h2>The New Priority List<\/h2>\n<p>Now I&#8217;m thinking that I need to focus more on the big picture with the business. It&#8217;s easy to get caught doing all kinds of little things each day. They\u00a0<em>have<\/em> to get done or at least you tell yourself that and you get so busy that you don&#8217;t have any time left for anything else.<\/p>\n<p>Sometimes those unwanted items can even get so routine that you become good at the. And that&#8217;s a bad case scenario because now you&#8217;re going to do those because it&#8217;s easy for you.<\/p>\n<p>Instead of doing that my focus now is to <em><strong>do the things I have to do today that will save me time in the future<\/strong><\/em>.<\/p>\n<p>And to focus on the things\u00a0today that will <em><strong>get more sales tomorrow<\/strong><\/em>.<\/p>\n<p>Yes, I still have to prioritize making sure fires are put out and that we have a good product. But I&#8217;m having a longer-term focus on those things and on the business.<\/p>\n<p>Here&#8217;s an example, we upload posts for most of our clients to their blog. We format and schedule the posts. I can do that in about 10 minutes each time. It&#8217;s easy for me to quickly do that. I&#8217;m good at it.<\/p>\n<p>It takes me an hour to document the procedure to do that for each client. Most use WordPress so the procedure is similar, but there are always little differences.<\/p>\n<p>The old way of thinking would be to just do it. But creating a procedure and having the writer upload it each week saves me a ton of time in the future.<\/p>\n<p>Here&#8217;s another example, I respond to a lot of emails each day. Well, it&#8217;s probably not too bad, but it&#8217;s a good portion of my dad. I&#8217;m working on shutting down the email and replying at only two times each day. I would like to cut that back to even once per day if I could.<\/p>\n<p>I could respond to those emails, which are inquiries, questions from current customers and questions from writers. Those are all very important.<\/p>\n<p>But more important is growing the business or thinking about ways to make the business and the service better. So I&#8217;m prioritizing bigger picture things first now. Or at least trying too.<\/p>\n<h2>Starting The Day With Something Fun<\/h2>\n<p>And I&#8217;ve found that if I start the day with something fun that I&#8217;m in a better frame of mind. I like doing crosswords in the morning. I like reading a chapter in a book or reading some <a href=\"http:\/\/longreads.com\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Longreads<\/a>.<\/p>\n<p>So I&#8217;ve been starting my day off that way. Then I get into The New Priority List. I&#8217;m still in the early stages, but things are getting more productive.<\/p>\n<p>And the more timely items still get done and people are still happy. I realized that a lot of the pressure that comes from the business I was putting on myself.<\/p>\n<p>The best entrepreneurs and business people I know seem to have the ability to think about the big picture. They will certainly do the unwanted work too, but only after they get more important things done.<\/p>\n<p>Maybe what I should have been doing back when I was a kid was thinking about how I could save time in the long run with those chores. Maybe spend an hour brainstorming a faster way to rake the yard instead of just doing it.<\/p>\n<p>Oh well, I&#8217;m hopefully learning that lesson now and I think it will lead to good things.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"<p>So I used to think it was best to do the most unwanted thing first in the morning.
